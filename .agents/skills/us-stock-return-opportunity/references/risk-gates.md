# Risk Gates

Use before final label. These gates control the final decision even when upside looks attractive.

## Hard Gates

Avoid or downgrade if any hard gate is triggered:

- Liquidity, solvency, or refinancing risk is unclear.
- Reported profit is not supported by operating cash flow without a credible reason.
- Valuation requires a best-case scenario with little margin of safety.
- Leverage is needed for the idea to be attractive.
- Thesis depends mainly on analyst target prices, social narrative, or price momentum.
- Material dilution risk is present and not compensated by valuation.
- Business quality is deteriorating while stock price rises.
- User wants to average down but original thesis no longer holds.

## Bubble Signals

- Price rises much faster than fundamentals.
- Narrative broadens from specific evidence to totalizing claims.
- Valuation multiple expands while estimates are flat or falling.
- Volatility and volume spike without primary-source confirmation.
- Peers rerate together on the same loose theme.

## Position Rules

- New entry: prefer staged buying and explicit invalidation.
- Existing holding: separate hold logic from add-more logic.
- High uncertainty: `Watch` or `Increase Cash` is valid.
- Strong upside but high downside: cap position size or wait.
