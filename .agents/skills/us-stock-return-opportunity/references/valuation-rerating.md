# Valuation Rerating

Use when upside depends on multiple expansion, peer discount closing, or a new valuation regime.

## Rerating Conditions

Rerating is more credible when at least one condition is true:

- Growth durability improves and becomes less cyclical.
- Margins structurally expand.
- FCF conversion improves.
- Balance sheet risk falls.
- Segment mix shifts toward higher-quality revenue.
- Market previously misclassified the company.
- Peer discount exists for a reason that is now fading.

## Overheating Checks

- Multiple already above own history and peers.
- Price rises faster than estimate revisions.
- Analyst targets chase price rather than new evidence.
- Narrative expands while reported cash flow lags.
- Retail/option activity dominates evidence.

## Output Rules

- Compare current multiple with history and peers.
- Explain why the multiple should change, not only why earnings may grow.
- If both earnings growth and multiple expansion are required, mark the setup higher risk.
- If valuation is already extreme, final label should usually be `Watch`, `Trim`, or `Avoid` unless evidence is exceptional.
