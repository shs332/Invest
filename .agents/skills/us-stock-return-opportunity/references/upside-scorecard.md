# Upside Scorecard

Use this after primary evidence and financial quality checks. Do not score upside from narrative alone.

## Core Checks

| Area | What To Check | Positive Evidence | Warning Signs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth durability | Revenue growth, backlog, guidance, segment trends | Multi-quarter growth with visible drivers | One-off rebound or price-led story |
| Margin expansion | Gross margin, operating margin, scale benefit | Operating leverage without quality decline | Margin improvement from temporary cuts only |
| FCF conversion | Operating cash flow, capex, SBC, working capital | FCF grows with earnings | EPS up but cash flow weak |
| Balance sheet capacity | Cash, debt, interest burden, dilution | Net cash or manageable debt | Refinancing, dilution, covenant pressure |
| Moat or edge | Switching cost, network, cost curve, IP, brand | Pricing power or share gain | Commodity exposure with no edge |
| Rerating path | Multiple versus peers and history | Clear reason for discount to close | Multiple already prices best case |
| Catalyst timing | Earnings, guidance, product cycle, macro change | Catalyst within visible window | Catalyst vague or repeatedly delayed |
| Relative strength | Trend, drawdown recovery, volume support | Strength with improving fundamentals | Parabolic move without evidence |

## Scoring

- `Strong`: multiple areas improving, downside bounded, catalyst visible.
- `Medium`: upside path exists, but valuation, timing, or evidence is incomplete.
- `Weak`: upside mostly narrative, financial quality unclear, or risk gate triggered.

Never let `Strong` upside override a failed risk gate.
